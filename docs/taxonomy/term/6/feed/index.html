The Chain of Lakes, the Mississippi River, and 200 miles of walking and biking trails are within city limits. Steps away are a vibrant theater and music scene, the best shopping in the region, and museums that are among the finest in the country. Below is a sampling of things to do and see in the Minneapolis area.</p> <ol> <li><a href="https://www.mallofamerica.com"><strong>Mall of America</strong></a> Largest U.S. mall and #1 tourist destination in U.S.</li> <li><a href="http://new.artsmia.org/"><strong>Minneapolis Institute of Art</strong></a> Traditional and Asian art may be seen at the MIA, one of the largest art museums in the country.</li> <li><a href="http://www.walkerart.org/"><strong>Walker Art Center</strong></a> and <a href="https://www.walkerart.org/garden/"><strong>Minneapolis Sculpture Garden</strong></a> Contemporary art</li> <li><a href="http://www.asimn.org/"><strong>American Swedish Institute</strong></a>&nbsp;This&nbsp;museum and cultural center&nbsp;is located in the historic Turnblad Castle.</li> <li><a href="http://www.millcitymuseum.org/"><strong>Mill City Museum</strong></a> Built in the historic ruins of a flour mill, Mill City Museum provides a history of MN grain milling and the Pillsbury and General Mills companies.</li> <li><a href="http://tmora.org/"><strong>The Museum of Russian Art</strong></a> Minneapolis is home to the only Russian art museum in North America.</li> <li><a href="http://www.guthrietheater.org/"><strong>Guthrie Theater</strong></a>&nbsp;This world renowned regional theater presents&nbsp;live plays on three stages and features a 178-foot cantilevered bridge overlooking the Mississippi River.</li> <li><a href="https://www.childrenstheatre.org/"><strong>Children’s Theater Company</strong></a> is recognized as the best theater for young audience and families in the U.S.</li> <li><a href="http://www.bravenewworkshop.com/"><strong>Brave New Workshop</strong></a>&nbsp;is a local favorite for satirical comedy sketch and improvisation and it's where Senator Al Franken and Louie Anderson got their start in comedy.</li> <li><a href="https://hennepintheatretrust.org/"><strong>Hennepin Avenue Theater District</strong></a> Orpheum, State, Pantages Theaters, and Cowles Center for Dance provide stages for&nbsp;plays, concerts, and other performing arts by national and local artists.</li> <li><a href="http://minnesota.twins.mlb.com/min/ballpark/"><strong>Target Field</strong></a> Minnesota Twins baseball field</li> <li><a href="https://www.minneapolisparks.org/parks__destinations/parks__lakes/minneapolis_chain_of_lakes_regional_park/"><strong>Chain of Lakes</strong></a> Lake Calhoun, Lake Harriet, and the Lake of the Isles are joined by trails to provide outdoor recreation in the form of&nbsp;walking, biking, rollerblading, canoeing, sailing, or windsurfing as well as&nbsp;lakeside restaurants and music clubs.</li> <li><a href="https://www.minneapolisparks.org/parks__destinations/parks__lakes/minnehaha_regional_park/"><strong>Minnehaha Regional Park</strong></a> features a natural 53-foot waterfall with picnic areas, music, restaurants.</li> <li><a href="https://www.niceridemn.org/"><strong>Nice Ride Bikes</strong></a> Rental bikes are available at stations throughout the Twin Cities.</li> <li><a href="http://www.mnzoo.org/"><strong>Minnesota Zoo</strong></a> features local and global animals – weekly summer concerts with national performers.</li> <li><a href="http://www.midtownglobalmarket.org/"><strong>Midtown Global Market</strong></a> was created to assist immigrants and emerging entrepreneurs in building a business involving foods, arts, crafts, or other aspects of their heritage – 40 businesses share this location. &nbsp;</li> <li><strong>Award-winning Restaurants</strong> Scandinavian, American, European, Asian, Middle-Eastern food&nbsp;</li> <li><strong>Music Venues</strong> <ul> <li><a href="http://www.dakotacooks.com/"><strong>The Dakota</strong></a> mounts nightly concerts by national and local performers.</li> <li><a href="http://www.minnesotaorchestra.org/"><strong>Orchestra Hall</strong></a> is home to the award-winning Minnesota Orchestra.</li> <li><a href="http://first-avenue.com/"><strong>First Avenue</strong></a> is a venue made famous by Prince.</li> </ul> </li> </ol> <p>St. Paul, the other half of the Twin Cities and the state capitol of Minnesota, is across the river and easily accessible by light rail for $1.75 - $2.25.</p> <ol> <li><a href="http://www.ordway.org/"><strong>Ordway Theater for Performing Arts</strong></a>&nbsp;hosts theater, dance, music, and other performing arts events and is home of the world class St. Paul Chamber Orchestra.</li> <li><a href="http://www.smm.org/"><strong>Science Museum of Minnesota</strong></a> offers multiple exhibits and 90-foot Omnitheater for films.</li> <li><a href="http://www.mcm.org/"><strong>Minnesota Children’s Museum</strong></a> has hands-on exhibits that are great for kids.</li> <li><a href="http://fitzgeraldtheater.publicradio.org/"><strong>Fitzgerald Theater</strong></a> is home to Prairie Home Companion hosted by Chris Thiele.</li> <li><a href="http://www.minnesotahistorycenter.org/"><strong>Minnesota History Center</strong></a> is an interactive museum with MN history exhibits.</li> <li><a href="http://penumbratheatre.org/"><strong>Penumbra Theater</strong></a> One of three African American theaters in the country, Penumbra launched the career of the late Pulitzer Prize winning playwright August Wilson.</li> <li><a href="http://www.stpaul.gov/departments/parks-recreation/harriet-island-regional-park"><strong>Harriet Island</strong></a> is the home of the Minnesota Centennial Showboat, a theater on a paddleboat docked in the Mississippi that features U of M students.</li> <li><a href="http://www.comozooconservatory.org/"><strong>Como Zoo and Conservatory</strong></a> features a free zoo and beautiful flower exhibits in the middle of a vast&nbsp;park.</li> <li><strong><a href="http://saintsbaseball.com/">CHS Field</a> – St. Paul Saints</strong> Minor league baseball team co-owned by actor Bill Murray. &nbsp;The team slogan is “Fun is Good.”</li> <li><a href="http://sites.mnhs.org/historic-sites/james-j-hill-house/summit-avenue-walking-tour"><strong>Historic Summit Avenue</strong></a>&nbsp;is a 4-mile stretch of mansions built 1890-1920. &nbsp;The James J. Hill House is a National Historic Landmark with tours. James J. Hill built the Great Northern Railway.</li> <li><a href="http://wabashastreetcaves.com"><strong>Wabasha Street Caves</strong></a>&nbsp;is a former 1930’s gangster nightclub&nbsp;built into the sandstone caves located on the south shore of the Mississippi River. &nbsp;</li> <li><a href="http://sites.mnhs.org/historic-sites/minnesota-state-capitol"><strong>Minnesota State Capitol</strong></a> Built in 1905 and designed by Cass Gilbert, our state capitol building is considered an architectural masterpiece; it boasts&nbsp;the second largest self-supporting marble dome in the world.</li>
